How Study Time Relates to Academic Success

This visualization uses real data from the OpenIntro GPA and Study Hours dataset , which surveyed 193 college students about their weekly study habits and grade point averages (GPAs). The scatter plot below shows a mild positive correlation: as students report studying more hours per week, their GPA tends to be higher.

"Students who commit steady time to coursework each week see measurable academic gains — but the effect plateaus beyond a certain point."

The data comes from OpenIntro, a nonprofit educational project providing real datasets for statistics learning. You can explore the full dataset here. This example illustrates how real-world data can confirm the intuition that consistent effort pays off.
